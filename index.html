01 · The Obsession

How it started

Ever since I was a teenager, I've been obsessed with taking notes. Whenever a lyric in a song connected to a scene in a movie, or a line in a book shed light on a real-life problem, I had to write it down.

I wanted to capture how the things I consumed were shaping how I saw the world.

02 · The Problem

The tools were broken

A note about a movie stayed trapped in a "Movies" folder. An insight from a book stayed isolated in a "Reading" folder. These tools forced my thoughts into rigid boxes.

They completely ignored the fact that human thought doesn't work in straight lines — it works in webs. The unexpected links between completely different topics were getting lost in the digital clutter.

03 · The Vision

A different way

I wanted a tool that worked the way our brains actually do. A tool where a thought about a philosophy book could collide with a concept from a sci-fi film to spark a brand-new idea.

Something built for synthesizers — not organizers.

04 · The Map

That's why I built Synapse

Synapse is the tool I wish I had a decade ago. It's designed for the curious minds and avid readers who know that true intelligence isn't about memorizing isolated facts — it's about seeing how everything connects.

True intelligence isn't a walking encyclopedia of isolated facts —
it's the web you weave between them.

Neuroscientists have found that highly creative and intelligent minds show high semantic connectivity. When they think of a concept, it doesn't activate one isolated node — it fires a massive, sprawling network of distant memories, analogies, and associations.

They see the link because their neural pathways are deeply interconnected. Synapse is built on this same principle — giving every reader a tool that thinks the way brilliant minds do.

Features

Everything your reading notes deserve

Visual Knowledge Graph
Watch your ideas come to life in a force-directed graph. Tap any node to explore connections you didn't know existed between your notes.
Wikilink Connections
Type [[Note title]] to link ideas instantly. Synapse builds the connections for you — no manual wiring required.
Organized by Book
Keep notes anchored to their source, with chapters and topics. Then break free — links jump across books to reveal cross-pollinating ideas.
Instant Search
Real-time search across all your notes, with highlighted matches. Find the idea you half-remember in under a second.
Private by Default
Your notes live locally on your device. Nothing leaves without your permission — no accounts required to get started.
Export Anywhere
Your knowledge, your format. Export your full graph as JSON whenever you want — no lock-in, ever.

How it works

From reading to insight in three steps

1
Add a book and take notes
Create a note for any idea that strikes you while reading. Assign it to a book and optional chapter to keep things organized.
2
Link ideas with [[wikilinks]]
Mention another note by wrapping its title in double brackets. Synapse detects these references and builds edges in your graph automatically.
e.g. [[Identity-based habits]]
3
Explore the graph
Open Graph View to see your entire knowledge network. Pan, zoom, tap nodes to reveal previews, and double-tap to dive deep into any idea.

Connections you craft.
Connections you never saw coming.

Linking ideas manually is itself an act of thinking — it forces you to understand why two concepts belong together. But some of the most powerful connections live between notes you wrote months apart, from books you read in different seasons of life. A Synapse AI Agent reads your entire knowledge base and surfaces the links your conscious mind skipped.

Suggested connections
The agent scans across all your notes — books, films, thoughts — and proposes links with an explanation of why they belong together.
Reasoning, not just a link
Every suggestion includes a "Because:" — a short explanation grounded in your own notes, not generic AI filler.
You stay in control
Accept a suggestion to add it to your graph. Dismiss it if it doesn't resonate. The AI is your co-pilot — the connections are always yours.
